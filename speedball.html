Part of a prototype for a redesigned lineup screen.

The lineup editing area was one of the most complex in the game, there was a challenge to show essential player information to users in a digestible way, to allow them to quickly balance player injuries, stats and preferred positions going into matches. This screen saw a lot of iteration!

Early prototype showing connections between areas.

Throughout the project meta game complexity was experimented with a lot and this was one option earlier on in development, which allowed in depth management in various areas for each one of the teams players.

Early mockups of in game player indicator plans.

Speedball is by design a fast paced and chaotic game so it was important to prioritise the key elements to always remain visible (or not!). Cross departmental collaboration was essential here - and lots of iteration and playtesting too.

Video of a prototype showing options for microinteractions and animations, and 3 high level style guides based on previous explorations from UI art.

I used Figma to aid the art team throughout the project in visualising animations and screen compositions, and brought intial art explorations into interactive style guides fit for presentation purposes.
